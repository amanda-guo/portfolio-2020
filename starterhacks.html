Project Overview

I was a Market Designer on the StarterHacks 2020 Organizing Committee. StarterHacks is Canada’s largest beginner-focused hackathon based at the University of Waterloo. I worked with a team of 6 other Market Designers to create graphics that were released to over 2500 potential hackers.

Responsibilities and Challenges

We worked both remotely and in person to complete a variety of design projects. Notably some of the larger ones included designing the Sponsorship Package, establishing the StarterHacks 2020 branding for the upcoming year and designing marketing/graphic material. Our main communication platform was Slack and we scheduled weekly meetings for tasks.

One of our biggest challenges (and successes) of the year was establishing a new brand. This was important because it was the first medium that we used to communicate to hackers and show them the hackathon’s theme. In 2020, we focused on adventure. Thus, our new branding had to reflect that theme. Essentially, we wanted to create excitement for the hackathon to get as many applicants as possible.

We have always believed in helping beginners break into the tech industry, and by offering them mentorship, guidance and support, we are there to help anyone learn new skills.

Of course, we wanted to show them that tech is friendly, but also focus on our specialty of segmenting tech into three key streams: Development, Design and Business. This became a challenge for us as we had to make sure our new branding reflected all the values that StarterHacks stood for.

Research

First off, we conducted some research to see what other hackathons were doing and how they were marketing themselves. This was to ensure that we would create a unique appearance for StarterHacks and gain an understanding of what made other hackathons’ branding successful in reflecting their own respective values. This helped us understand our goal for branding on a high level.

Ideation

Next, we began to do many brainstorming sessions to establish the branding. We came up with a variety of ideas, and tried to see how each would look in a “Save the Date” post that announced the hackathon date. We found that many of our early ideas were not scalable to several marketing campaigns or lacked a key aspect of our branding that was crucial to communicate.

Feedback and Iteration

During the brainstorming process we often used Figma’s comment feature and it helped us communicate our thoughts and ideas with each other on how to improve the branding as it was being established. This was key in making sure that everyone on the team was working towards the same goal and being honest with each other’s work.

Final Designs

In a final big brainstorm session, we choose several landmarks around the world (the CN Tower, the Statue of Liberty and Big Ben) and decided to take some of the elements of these landmarks and transform them into various aspects of Development, Design and Business. We also supplemented these three landmarks with cute characters to convey the friendly atmosphere to potential hackers. In the end, we received almost 3000 applications and had 1000 hackers attend our event. Reflection

I learned so much from this experience. Not only did I hone in on my Figma skills, but I got to collaborate with a group of talented designers. We often bounced ideas off one another and that’s often where our best ideas came from - teamwork.

I learned that it won’t always be my idea that became final. When we did our brainstorming sessions, it became an opportunity for me to present some thoughts I had, and then as a team we would iron out the details. Working with a team of designers offered a variety of perspectives - especially on how to approach a problem we were facing. This was key to our success as a group.

Finally, Figma’s comment feature is amazing and it allowed us to clearly communicate as a team when collaborating together. It was due to this feedback (that was asked and implemented often as we iterated through our designs) that reflected how the whole truly is greater than the sum of its parts.
